Norton 360 for Gamers – Content Strategy
Role

Launching Norton 360 for Gamers was one of the first projects I worked on copy for while at Norton. Our team so was small, so, in addition to UI/UX content design, part of my role was developing copy for the product as a whole. That included the content that would be displayed on the Norton homepage and in other promotional materials. You can see the copy I wrote on the Norton homepage today in the descriptions of features for Norton 360 and FAQs on their homepage. The copy and guidelines I created were vetted by the legal department and immortalized in a style guide for future writers.

Ecommerce – Corporate Blog, Press, & Social Media
Role

My role at Seville Classics as their copywriter was comprehensive, as is typical of a small business. Part of my role was managing the communication segment of angles of the acquisition funnel. Seville Classics had hired a creative agency as a partner, and my role was to manage their content. I was in charge of the editorial calendar. I made keyword and topic recommendations to our agency according to our analytics. I edited their articles, emails, and press releases to make sure they were accurate.

Ultimately, for Seville Classics, improving the search engine position for the homepage through content marketing was not effective quickly enough for ownership.

Email marketing, however, proved useful. Even after the relationship ended with our agency, I and a graphic designer continued to regularly publish email blasts and newsletters to drive sales.

Internet Brands Automotive Group – Blog Content Manager
Role

I was hired to Internet Brands as the second editor to their automotive content group. My role was to develop blog materials for Internet Brands forum homepages in order to increase traffic for improved advertising revenue. The key performance indicators for success in our case were page views, clicks, and shares. The strategy was relatively straightforward at first, but became a refined process to increase output fairly quickly.

The first step was to identify the audience for each automotive property as they all had different demographics (Ford-Trucks.com folk are not ClubLexus.com folk.) The second was to identify trends in popular content from the forums and other social media. Third, was to setup alerts and daemons to gather news daily from various sources to ensure our content was up to date and somewhat news worthy.

Then we’d created posts and schedule them on an editorial calendar, review the analytics, and iterate with new content. We were successful enough that several freelance editors and an additional in-house editor position was added. These pages are still popular and have managed to breathe continuous new life into the aging forum platform.
