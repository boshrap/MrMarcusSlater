Norton Portal - Merged Account Picker
Role

My role in this project was to revise the existing login flow to appropriately sort users with multiple accounts tied to the same username/email address. NortonLifeLock's userbase had swollen due to acquisitions of Avira, Bullguard, Avast and LifeLock. Many users had more than one account tied to the same email address due to joining more than one service prior to the merger. Complicating the matter, users were also allowed to create accounts through third-party sign in services like Google and Apple ID—accounts that were not tied to their existing subscription—creating a situation where they were likely to accidentially sign in to an inactive account.

Our tiger-team trio (UX visual, content, and research) analyzed the flow and collaborated with engineering to create an unobtrusive and technically-lightweight solution: an account picker. Our challenge was in developing the labrynth of flows to safely and accurately guide users to their intended accounts based on their point of origin and entered information.

The login flow, and the third-party account flow, were major pain points for users according to analytics due to the empty account issue. Putting these fixes into place resulted in a significant decrease in complaints, and the elimination of "no subscription found" errors in our analytics. We were quite successful and immediately rewarded with more work (the "Delete My Data" flow and the Restricted Persons List flow.)
