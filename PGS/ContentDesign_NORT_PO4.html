Norton Portal - Third-Part Sign In Flow
Role

Product changes relted to user accounts were assigned to our team. In this project, we were tasked with updating the login flow to be more secure for use with third-party accounts. As the content designer, I worked in the visual designer in mocking out the flow and annotating where we could remove steps, remove copy, and make the page design more intuitive.

The flow was triggered whenever a user would sign in with Google or Apple. We would use their information to determine if they already had an account with Norton or one of their merged sevices (Bullguard, Avira, LifeLock, etc.,) and proceed to sign them in. Otherwise, we would continue the flow to create a new Norton account using their selected Google or Apple accounts. It was a popular option that proved quite secure when paired with two-factor authentication.

This was a faily sophisticated flow on the back-end, though the final user experience was seamless. Norton had recently completed several mergers in addition to having a large body of older accounts with different rules for usernames. This project, and the Merged Accounts UI project, attempted to resolve technical issues with the user creation/sign-in process that had created security risks. Today, both third-party sign in and multi-account usernames work smoothly and safely regardless of which Gen Inc. app you use.
