Disney Cruise Line Port Adventure - Content Audit
Role

I was contracted to Walt Disney Parks and Resorts to edit the copy for the on-shore activities that cruise ship customers can purchase on their trip. The original copy had been compiled over the years, and the accompanying images and content lacked alt-text, captions, and a consistent style.

My role was to perform the following: 1) annotate the images lacking alt-text and captions. 2) rewrite section headers and titles to have a fun and friendly tone. 3) edit all content to comply with the Disney style guide.

While the group of editors they brought on touched each article, I extensively worked on the port adventures for Mexico, Alaska, and the Panama Canal.

Internet Brands Automotive Group – Editor-at-Large
Role

I wrote more than 200 blog posts across more a dozen sites during my time at Internet Brands. In addition, I also wrote content for email newsletters. Writing blog articles was the first task when I started Internet Brands, and it was the skill that got me hired in the first place.

We were successful almost immediately after starting. Despite some forum grumblings from users resitant to change, regular content increased traffic notably from email and social media. Moreover, users were staying on the pages longer for the purpose of browsing blog content: not just the forums. It also improved the potency of our newsletter content, increasing click-through rates and improving the value of our advertising.

Today, the groundwork we laid continues to bear fruit for Internet Brands. More important, those enthusiast pages are now home to automotive content writer from across the world who are able to write about the things their passionate about (like I did!) and get paid while doing it.
